A Collection of Memories from Life in Prague, CZ

Getting There - Trials, Nerves, Victory

Deciding to move to Prague, CZ was the easy part. I had recently graduated from Western State Colorado University in Gunnsion, CO and enjoyed the winter living and working as a ski bum. I was considering going to graduate school or travelling - my travel bug won out. I promptly found a TEFL course (Teaching English as a Foreign Language) and booked a one way ticket to the ancient fairytale city.

I flew into Amsterdam and had a few hours to marvel in the new land beneath my feet before embarking on my first test of this solo adventure. I had never been to Europe before but had decided that I would rather navigate the train systems rather than fly straight into Prague - because why do things the easy way? The trains don't provide a lot of space to move around and are definitely not ideal for travelling with large luggage. I'm sure I many people had a chuckle as they watched this naive American girl lug two fifty pound suitcases, a backpack, and a yoga mat across three train platforms. I honestly have no idea how I managed to hop on the correct trains but with some help from friendly strangers and sheer stubborness I made it to my new home, Prague.

TEFL with The Language House Prague

Many young Americans who want to travel and work take a TEFL course so that they are able to live and work around the world. The course that I chose was through The Language House Prague. It was an intense month long course and way harder than I imagined. I chose this specific school because they help students get settled into their new life in the city by providing housing during the course and helped students find housing and jobs once they completed the program. They also helps people navigate applying for a work visa and were an overall great support system to have in a foreign land.

I completed the program and had intentions of living and teaching in Prague for one year. When I realized that I would have to work 40-50 hours a week to make ends meet. I didn't cross the Atlantic to work more than I did at home. I wanted to eplore as much as possible and that became my number one priority. I made the decision to cut my trip short so that I could focus on exploring and enjoying life abroad. I tutored a few individuals and families for beer and food money (and to not completely drain my savings) but overall focused on where my next adventure would be.

The Wonders of the Czech Republic

Prague - A Fairy Tale City

Cobblestone streets. Castles. Giant babies. The Charles Bridge. Giant city parks. Red roofs. Extravagent churches. Beer cheaper than water. Public transport to hiking trails. And too many hidden gems and alleyways to count or share. This city will always have a special place in my heart. It held space for me to expand, grow, and rediscover myself.

Beyond Prague

Prague is by far one of the most popular travel destinations in Europe which leads to people overlooking the rest of what the country has to offer. I am very grateful that I was able to explore beyond the city walls of Prague.
